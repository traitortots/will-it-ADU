Welcome to: ADU Opportunities - How accessory dwelling units can create economic opportunity and address the housing crisis in American college towns

A project by Caleb Smith and Lancelot Comrie for CRP 5850: Mapping and Countermapping at Cornell University in Fall 2022.

This project identifies locations suitable for the development of Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) by using publicly available tax parcel and building footprint GIS data in Python to conduct a geometric assessments of the available space in individual parcels to determine whether there is sufficient space available for the construction of an ADU.

We believe that this project will reveal an untapped potential for neighborhood densification through the targeted development of accessory dwelling units in Ithaca, New York,and New Haven, Connecticut, which would show that ADUs are a viable way to address the housing crisis in these two cities while remaining sensitive to neighborhood context and alleviating common complaints about the production of new housing in communities. This project will also show the various layers of feasibility faced by those who would like to build ADUs on their properties and/or communities.

Neighborhood Context (Ithaca)
In Ithaca, 73% of households are renters due to the large student community as a result of Cornell University and Ithaca College being situated in the city. Additionally, there is a low supply of housing in the city which has made existing properties very expensive and almost unreachable to the average family.The city is predominately white but with significant populations of African Americans in the West Hill neighborhoods of the city. In these neighborhoods, many residents are experiencing poverty and sharp structural racism at the hands of incompetent landlords. Ithaca population - 31,710 (2011)
